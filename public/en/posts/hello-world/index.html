The Whitespace of Architecture: Designing Systems Like West Lake

Two years ago, I was obsessed with breaking everything down. It was the peak of the microservices craze, and I believed that only through extreme decoupling could I handle what people called “business explosion.” So I took a monolith that worked just fine and split it into dozens of independent services.

“Complexity rarely comes from the business logic itself — it comes from the rules we impose on top of it.”

The result was predictable: network latency, distributed transactions, service governance complexity growing geometrically. The team spent every day firefighting infrastructure issues while the actual business logic stagnated.

Natural Architecture

Withered lotus flowers don’t exist for beauty — they’re the natural result of a life cycle. Architecture is the same. It shouldn’t be a preordained perfect model, but a texture that grows with the business.

// Intuition-driven approach
function solveProblem(data: unknown) {
  if (!data) return null;
  return processDirectly(data);
}

Now I lean toward Vibe Coding. Rather than starting with design patterns, I write flowing code first. When pain points emerge, when logic starts to repeat, when the urge to refactor rises from intuition — that’s when architecture naturally comes into being.

The shimmering light on West Lake isn’t by anyone’s design. It’s just the wind and the sun doing their thing. Code is the same.
